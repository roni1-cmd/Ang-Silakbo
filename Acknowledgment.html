Personal Reflection

Ron Asnahon

Student, CP12A1

As I pause to reflect on the path that has led me to where I stand today, I am overwhelmed with gratitude for the myriad experiences, challenges, and opportunities that have shaped my academic journey and personal development. This acknowledgment serves as a testament to the invaluable contributions of individuals, institutions, and circumstances that have guided and supported me throughout my time with Ang Silakbo. The tapestry of experiences woven throughout this journey has created a rich foundation upon which I continue to build, each thread representing a lesson learned, a relationship formed, or a challenge overcome.

The road has not always been straightforward, yet each detour and obstacle has ultimately contributed to a richer, more meaningful experience. I have come to appreciate that success is rarely a solitary achievement but rather the culmination of collaborative efforts, mentorship, and the generous support of those who believe in our potential. Through moments of triumph and periods of uncertainty, the constant has been the unwavering support of a community that values growth, creativity, and authentic expression.

My Personal Journey with Ang Silakbo

Looking back at my time with Ang Silakbo fills me with nostalgia and gratitude (LOL last year pa sha but HAHAHAHHA ayaw na lang ijudge). What began as an opportunity to hone my broadcasting skills evolved into a transformative experience that shaped both my abilities and personal connections. The journey, with its unexpected turns and serendipitous encounters, has been instrumental in defining not only my professional trajectory but also my understanding of community, purpose, and personal excellence. Each chapter of this story has contributed to a narrative far richer than I could have anticipated when I first embarked on this path.

The Time I Joined As A Broadcaster

I was a broadcaster at that time, and I knew it would be a great opportunity to hone my skills. When I got accepted into Ang Silakbo, it felt like a door opening to new possibilities. I remember the excitement and nervousness I felt, knowing this was my chance to grow. The initial days were filled with a mixture of anticipation and uncertainty as I navigated unfamiliar territory, learning the rhythms and expectations of this new environment. Despite the challenges of adapting to new responsibilities and relationships, there was an underlying sense of purpose that propelled me forward, a conviction that this path, however uncertain, was aligned with my aspirations and values.

First Silak-View Experience

My first Silak-View was both challenging and exhilarating, I was with Kharl Jones Almacin at that time with Eirene Bardago as the video journalist and Jolisa Marie Yabillo as our video editor. I remembered we interviewed Rhoda Amistad that time who eventually became a broadcaster (kahinumdom pa ko ani kadtong gipost kaduha among silakview kay naay nasulti si Rhoda nga wa nako ka trace), too. I put all my effort into making it a success, learning so much about broadcasting techniques, audience engagement, and teamwork in the process. This experience confirmed that I had made the right decision in joining Ang Silakbo. The preparation was intense, requiring hours of research, script development, and rehearsal. There were moments of doubt and frustration, times when the task seemed overwhelming, but the collaborative spirit of the team and the guidance of experienced mentors provided the support needed to persevere. The day of the broadcast brought a surge of adrenaline, a test of all we had prepared, and ultimately, a profound sense of accomplishment that transcended the technical success of the program.

Division Schools Press Conference

A few weeks later, I got in for the Division Schools Press Conference. We practiced and practiced tirelessly, refining our skills and preparing for the competition. I will never forget these names; Joemelle Surilla, Rachel Ochea, Jan Ivan Alolod, Yaneca Marie Arong, Samantha Verdun, and Jolisa Maria Yabillo. During this time, I made many friends in the English broadcasting team. The bonds we formed through shared challenges and late-night practice sessions became one of the most valuable aspects of this experience. The intensity of preparation created a unique camaraderie, a shared understanding that could only come from navigating the pressures and aspirations together. We critiqued each other's work, celebrated improvements, and supported one another through moments of self-doubt. The competition itself, while important, became secondary to the growth and relationships that emerged from the process.

"Even if we lost the competition, I have my friends which is more precious than any merits I could receive. The connections we forged in those challenging moments have proven more enduring and meaningful than any accolade could ever be."

The Idea

When I first joined the team, I applied and then received an email confirmation. I doubted that it was automated—it was actually manually sent. Seeing an opportunity for improvement, I approached Ms. Queenie to propose that I could create a website for seamless operations. We agreed this would be beneficial for everyone. This moment represented a pivotal shift in my journey, a transition from simply participating in existing structures to actively contributing to their evolution. The idea emerged from a genuine desire to address a practical challenge, but it ultimately opened a door to a new dimension of my involvement with Ang Silakbo, one that would draw on different skills and offer new avenues for impact.

I remember sitting down with Ms. Queenie, nervously explaining my idea. Her enthusiasm and support gave me the confidence to pursue this project, showing me that good ideas are valued here. The conversation was a lesson in the power of initiative and the importance of environments that nurture innovation. What could have been dismissed as unnecessary or ambitious was instead recognized for its potential value, a testament to the culture of growth and improvement that permeates Ang Silakbo. This early vote of confidence became a foundation for the work that followed, a reminder that meaningful change often begins with a simple observation and the courage to suggest a better way.

Promotion

In the middle of the website development, I was promoted from broadcaster to production team head. I was happy about this recognition of my work, but also felt a bit sad for someone who had been demoted. This taught me an important lesson about leadership and the complexities of organizational changes. The promotion brought new responsibilities and challenges, requiring a shift in perspective from individual contributor to team leader. It demanded a balance between driving the project forward and supporting the needs and growth of team members. The mixed emotions I experienced—pride in the recognition yet empathy for a colleague's disappointment—highlighted the human dimension of organizational decisions, a complexity that textbooks and training programs often fail to capture adequately.

Expansion

What started as a simple tool for automated email sharing grew into something much more comprehensive. The development process was exciting as the website expanded and expanded until it became a full-stack web application that everyone loved. What began as a simple automation tool evolved to include articles, community features similar to social networks, and much more. It transformed from a dedicated automated email sharing system into a multifunctional web application capable of handling virtually anything. This evolution was not planned from the outset but emerged organically through continuous feedback, observation of needs, and a willingness to experiment and iterate. Each new feature or improvement was a response to a real challenge or opportunity, grounded in the actual experiences and requirements of the community it served.

"Seeing everyone use and appreciate something I created was one of the most rewarding experiences of my journey. There is a unique satisfaction in witnessing your work make a tangible difference in the lives of others, in knowing that your efforts have contributed to a more efficient, connected, and vibrant community."

Lessons Learned

The journey has taught me that success is not merely measured by achievements or accolades, but by the impact we have on others, the integrity with which we conduct ourselves, and the legacy we create through our work and relationships. It has reinforced the value of continuous learning, adaptability, and resilience in navigating the ever-evolving landscape. This perspective shifts the focus from external validation to intrinsic purpose, from temporary recognition to lasting contribution. It acknowledges that while achievements are important markers of progress, they are meaningful primarily as expressions of values and vehicles for impact. This understanding has influenced not only how I evaluate my own efforts but also how I recognize and celebrate the contributions of others, looking beyond visible accomplishments to appreciate the character, commitment, and care that underlie them.

I have learned that true fulfillment comes from alignment between our work and our values, from contributing to something larger than ourselves, and from the relationships we build along the way. The challenges encountered have taught me the importance of perseverance, the power of a positive mindset, and the necessity of self-care in sustaining long-term success. This insight highlights the interconnection between personal satisfaction and broader purpose, between individual well-being and collective contribution. It suggests that meaningful work is not defined solely by its content or outcomes but by its resonance with our core values and its connection to communities or causes that matter to us. The relationships formed through shared endeavors—the collaborations, mentorships, and friendships that develop in the context of purposeful work—are not incidental benefits but essential elements of a fulfilling experience, sources of support, growth, and joy that enrich both the process and the outcome.

Perhaps most importantly, this journey has taught me the significance of gratitude—of recognizing and acknowledging the contributions of others to our success, of appreciating the opportunities afforded to us, and of finding value in every experience, whether perceived as positive or negative at the time. Gratitude shifts our focus from what is lacking to what is present, from what we desire to what we have received, from entitlement to appreciation. It reminds us that our achievements are rarely solely our own but are built upon the support, guidance, and opportunities provided by others. This perspective fosters humility, strengthens relationships, and enhances well-being by connecting us to the abundance that exists even amidst challenges. The practice of gratitude—of consciously recognizing and expressing appreciation for the people, experiences, and resources that contribute to our journey—is not merely a social courtesy but a transformative habit that enriches our experience and deepens our impact.

"Gratitude turns what we have into enough, and more. It turns denial into acceptance, chaos into order, confusion into clarity... it makes sense of our past, brings peace for today, and creates a vision for tomorrow. The simple act of acknowledging the good in our lives—the support we've received, the opportunities we've been given, the lessons we've learned—transforms not only our perspective but our experience, revealing abundance where we might otherwise see scarcity and possibility where we might otherwise see limitation."

— Melody Beattie
