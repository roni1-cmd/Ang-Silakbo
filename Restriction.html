Membership Restriction Notice

Thank you for your interest in becoming a member of ANG SILAKBO. We appreciate your enthusiasm for contributing to our literary publication. However, we've identified that you are currently an active member of another sub-organization.

Here's why:

As a member of another sub-organization, you already have commitments that may conflict with Ang Silakbo's activities and deadlines.

  • Conflicting commitments: Being part of multiple organizations often leads to scheduling conflicts, especially during peak periods like events, competitions, and publication deadlines. This can result in divided attention, reduced quality of work, and increased stress for you.
  • Dedication requirements: Ang Silakbo requires dedicated members who can fully commit to our publication schedule, attend regular meetings, and meet strict deadlines. Our experience shows that students who try to balance multiple organizational commitments often struggle to fulfill their responsibilities to either group effectively.
  • Focus on current commitment: We encourage you to focus on your current organizational commitment. If you're still interested in contributing to Ang Silakbo in the future, you may consider applying after your term with your current organization ends.

This policy has been implemented based on years of experience and feedback from both organization advisors and students. We've found that allowing simultaneous memberships often results in burnout, missed deadlines, and decreased quality of contributions, which ultimately impacts the publication's overall quality and timeliness.

Frequently Asked Questions
Can I still submit my written work to Ang Silakbo?

Yes! We welcome literary submissions from all students, regardless of membership status. You can submit your poems, short stories, essays, and other creative works through our online submission portal for consideration in upcoming issues.

What if my role in my current organization isn't time-intensive?

While we understand that involvement levels vary, our policy applies to all official members of sub-organizations. However, you may request a special exemption review if you believe your circumstances warrant consideration. Each case is evaluated individually by our membership committee.

When can I apply to join Ang Silakbo?

You can apply immediately after your term with your current sub-organization ends. We hold recruitment drives at the beginning of each semester (August and January). If your term ends mid-semester, you may be eligible for our mid-year intake, subject to availability.

Is there any way to participate without being a full member?

You can participate as a contributor by submitting work, attending our public workshops and literary events, or volunteering for specific one-time projects that don't require ongoing commitment.

Can I appeal this decision?

Yes, appeals can be submitted to our faculty advisor with supporting documentation explaining your unique circumstances. While exceptions are rare, we do consider appeals on a case-by-case basis, particularly for students with exceptional literary talent or specialized skills needed by our publication.

Next Steps & Recommendations

We understand this may be disappointing news, but we have several recommendations for how you can proceed:

  • Complete your current term: Focus on making the most of your experience with your current sub-organization. The skills and connections you build there will be valuable regardless of future endeavors.
  • Stay connected: Subscribe to our newsletter and follow our social media to stay updated on Ang Silakbo's activities and submission opportunities.
  • Contribute as a non-member: Submit your literary works for consideration in our upcoming publications. Non-member submissions are a vital part of our diverse content.
  • Attend public events: Many of our workshops, readings, and literary discussions are open to the broader student body. Participating in these events can help you connect with our community.
  • Plan ahead: If joining Ang Silakbo remains a priority for you, consider planning your organizational commitments accordingly for next semester.
