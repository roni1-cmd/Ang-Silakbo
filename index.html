Our Commitments

Our Mission

To serve as a platform for students to voice their opinions, showcase their talents, and report on various events and achievements within the university.

Key Objectives:

  • Inform the public about student body actions
  • Report on university policies and current issues
  • Highlight student welfare programs
  • Cover official activities and achievements

Our Vision

To be the premier source of information and creative expression for the University of Cebu Lapu-Lapu and Mandaue Senior High School community.

Long-term Goals:

  • Foster a well-informed student body
  • Encourage student engagement in university affairs
  • Promote excellence in journalism and creative arts
  • Empower students through knowledge and expression

Our Values

  • Integrity in reporting
  • Creativity in expression
  • Respect for diversity
  • Commitment to student empowerment
  • Pursuit of excellence
  • Ethical journalism
  • Continuous learning and growth
  • Collaboration and teamwork

The New Year: A Time for Celebration and Compassion for Our Furry Friends

As the New Year preparations begin, the air fills with excitement and anticipation. Fireworks light up the sky, music reverberates through the streets, and celebrations echo from every corner. For us, it's a time of joy and festivity—a chance to welcome a new chapter with open arms.

However, amidst this exuberance, we often overlook a crucial perspective: the experience of our beloved pets. For our fur babies, the New Year's celebration can be a bewildering and terrifying ordeal. The sudden, deafening bursts of fireworks and the chaotic energy of celebrations are not festivities to them—they are sources of immense fear and confusion.

Without understanding the cause of the noise and chaos, many animals are left trembling, hiding, or exhibiting signs of stress. Their eyes, wide with fear, seem to plead for solace, for a sanctuary from the storm raging outside.

First youth service highlights belongingness and faith

>On January 24, 2025, the University of Cebu Lapu-Lapu and Mandaue (UCLM) Senior High School department hosted a youth service titled “Remember: Who You Are” at the Maritime AVR, organized by the Every Nation Campus (ENC) organization. The event began with board games and camaraderie among participants, followed by a prayer and worship led by the music team of ENC. Ms. Clouie Lastima shared the Parable of the Lost Son (Luke 15:11-32), emphasizing themes of repentance and unconditional love.

UCLM SHS Student's Preparation to Real World Environment

Immersion students prepared for their February deployment through a seminar organized with the support of their coordinators: Ms. Winna Tibon (GAS/Senior Academic Program Coordinator), Ms. Angelie Paquibot (HE), Merlou Camayra (ICT), and Genaine Pingil (HUMSS).
